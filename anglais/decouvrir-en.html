What’s Orienteering ?

Orienteering is an individual or a team sport that involves a succession of beacons that you have to find and validate, to do that you’ll have to find those beacons with a map and a compass, of course you’ll need to be fast but also have a good sense of direction. Depending on the race you’ll have to draw a route and prepare your stuff well.

Who is it for ?

Orienteering is open for everyone, however you have to choose your race wisely depending on the difficulty of this race, of course every race has a difficulty level but you also need to look at the race type ‘cause it’s an important indicator of the race’s difficulty. Knowing this, everyone can do orienteering, you just need to acknowledge your level and skills and choose your races according to them.

Why should you do it ?

First, orienteering is an excellent way to exercise and to keep in shape. Running will increase your cardio and your stamina but also make your brain work. Moreover, orienteering is the perfect hobby if you want to reconnect with nature as the majority of races take place in parks and forests.If you have the level you can participate in many official championships. Finally if you want to do a good deed some orienteering charity events are available.

Different type of orienteering

You can practice orienteering in a free way or with a predefined succession of beacons, you can also race at night therefore the required equipment can vary.

On foot

The most common version of orienteering, foot race can be divided in several categories : raids, sprint, relay, precision race.

- Sprint : The most common race is the sprint, you just have to do the best chrono.

- Relay : You and your team have to relay to complete the race.

- Raid : you and your pair have to work together in complete autonomy for a 2 days of racing on which you’ll have to manage your food and water.

- Precision race : this race is mostly practiced by disabled people (but not only), you don’t have to be quick but you’ll have to be careful, decoys have been placed to trick you, you'll have to look closely at your map to choose the real beacons.

MTB Orienteering

Orienteering can be practiced with a mtb either in a multisport race which includes different sports in one race but also less frequently in full mtb races.

Canoe Orienteering

Also in multisport som races include canoeing but it’s pretty uncommon.

Mountain Orienteering

You can also practice orienteering in snow, with ski or snowshoes you can search your beacons in the fresh air of a mountain.
