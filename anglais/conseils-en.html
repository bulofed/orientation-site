PREPARATION

First the orienteer must have the right outfit and of course good shoes because most of the races will feature some vegetation and steep paths. You will also need warm clothes in case of rain or any other weather change. Every race can require a different equipment, for example at night you’ll need a headlamp, a raid will require food and water that you will have to manage. A Finger Stick would also be useful if you join a club or participate in a championship, this device will allow you to confirm your beacons with just a scan but also to track you with an included chip.

MISTAKES TO AVOID

The first mistake you must avoid is to rush, choosing your route is an important step of the race and you cannot botch it. Moreover you’ll need to stay focused, a beginner can easily get lost even with a map and a compass and you could lose a lot of precious time. Try to stick a maximum to the route you chose. Finally take your time, find a good rhythm because orienteering isn’t about speed but mostly about stamina, you'll have to keep a rhythm to be efficient from the start to the end.

MAP AND COMPASS

Orienteering maps are not quite the same as common maps, they differ by their precision and their key that you will have to learn.To use the compass you’ll have to learn the azimuth: to find beacons you will sometimes have to follow a direction given by an azimuth. The azimuth is a value formed by the angle between north and the direction you have to follow.
